# Customer Discovery

Use this reference when the founder needs to validate a customer problem before building.

## Interview Goals

Discover:

- who has the problem;
- when it happens;
- what triggers action;
- how they solve it now;
- what the workaround costs;
- who owns budget or decision authority;
- what would make switching worthwhile.

## Interview Script

Start with context, not the product:

```text
What does your workflow look like today?
When does this problem show up?
What did you do the last time it happened?
What tools, people, or workarounds are involved?
What breaks if you do nothing?
How do you evaluate a better solution?
Who else is involved in the decision?
```

Avoid pitching until the founder has heard enough examples to understand the current workflow.

## Evidence Quality

Strong evidence:

- repeated stories from similar customers;
- current spend, manual labor, or urgent workaround;
- willingness to introduce peers or review a prototype;
- specific trigger events and constraints.

Weak evidence:

- "That sounds interesting";
- generic market enthusiasm;
- compliments from friends or advisors;
- interest without a painful current workaround.

## Synthesis Output

Produce:

- repeated pain themes;
- exact customer language;
- buying triggers;
- current alternatives;
- urgency score;
- recommended next interview segment.
