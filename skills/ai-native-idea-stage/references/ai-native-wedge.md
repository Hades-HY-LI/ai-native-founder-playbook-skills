# AI-Native Wedge

Use this reference to turn a broad idea into a narrow AI-native entry point.

## Wedge Criteria

A good wedge is:

- narrow enough to build quickly;
- painful enough to get attention;
- close to existing workflows;
- measurable in user outcomes;
- expandable after trust is earned.

## AI-Native Advantage Patterns

Look for one or more:

- turns unstructured input into structured action;
- reduces expert labor or coordination cost;
- personalizes work at low marginal cost;
- monitors, drafts, or executes recurring workflows;
- improves with workflow-specific feedback or data;
- enables a user who previously needed a specialist.

## Bad Wedge Signals

- "AI for everyone in this industry";
- value depends on replacing an entire system on day one;
- no clear human workflow to augment;
- no way to evaluate success;
- no data or feedback loop after use.
