# Opportunity Filter

Use this reference to decide whether an idea deserves MVP investment.

## Scorecard

Rate each item from 1 to 5:

| Dimension | Question |
| --- | --- |
| Pain | Is the problem frequent, expensive, urgent, or risky? |
| Reach | Can the founder reach this customer segment directly? |
| Budget | Is there a clear buyer or resource owner? |
| Workflow Fit | Does the solution fit where the problem already happens? |
| AI Leverage | Does AI change speed, quality, cost, personalization, or scale? |
| Wedge | Can the first version be narrow and valuable? |
| Defensibility | Can usage, data, workflow depth, trust, or distribution compound? |

## Decision Rules

Continue when:

- pain, reach, and wedge are all strong;
- customer language is specific;
- the MVP can prove one important workflow;
- AI meaningfully changes the solution.

Pause or revise when:

- the user is vague;
- the buyer is unclear;
- the idea depends on broad behavior change;
- the AI component is a thin feature;
- the first product requires too much surface area.
