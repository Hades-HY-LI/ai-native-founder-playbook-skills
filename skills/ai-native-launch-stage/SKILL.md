# AI-Native Launch Stage

## Goal

Help founders turn a working MVP into a focused launch motion that creates customers, feedback, revenue signals, and repeatable learning.

## Required Inputs

If the founder provides a structured brief, use these inputs:

```text
Product/MVP status:
Ideal customer profile:
Positioning hypothesis:
Current users or pipeline:
Pricing hypothesis:
Onboarding flow:
Acquisition channel:
Feedback signals:
Desired output:
```

## Guided Intake

Do not require the founder to provide a full launch brief. If the request is vague, ask up to five questions first:

```text
1. What product or MVP can users try now?
2. Who is the narrowest ideal customer for the first launch motion?
3. How are you currently reaching users or buyers?
4. What launch problem is most urgent: positioning, sales, pricing, onboarding, or feedback?
5. What evidence do you have from users, demos, pilots, or sales conversations?
```

After the user answers, choose the narrowest useful launch artifact and state assumptions. Ask follow-ups only if the launch audience or available product is still impossible to infer.

## Workflow

1. Clarify the launch audience and buying/user trigger.
2. Use `references/positioning.md` to convert product capability into customer value.
3. Use `references/founder-led-sales.md` to plan outreach, discovery, demos, follow-up, and close/learn loops.
4. Use `references/pricing-and-onboarding.md` to design a simple offer and activation path.
5. Use `references/launch-operating-system.md` to replace repetitive founder attention with AI-assisted workflows.
6. Return a launch plan with messaging, target list, sales motion, onboarding, feedback loops, and decision points.

## AI-Native Workflows

Use generic AI roles:

- Research assistant: build account lists and summarize buyer context.
- Sales copilot: draft outreach, discovery guides, demo scripts, and follow-ups.
- Customer-success assistant: create onboarding, FAQs, and activation nudges.
- Feedback analyst: cluster objections, usage notes, churn risks, and feature requests.
- Workflow agent: automate repeatable launch operations with human review.

## Exit Criteria

The Launch stage is complete when:

- a narrow ICP responds consistently;
- customers understand the positioning;
- onboarding reaches the promised outcome;
- pricing has evidence from real conversations or transactions;
- feedback loops identify what to improve next.

## Common Failure Modes

- Launching to everyone instead of a narrow ICP.
- Mistaking attention for demand.
- Automating sales before the founder understands the buyer.
- Pricing without value or urgency evidence.
- Collecting feedback without a decision process.

## Recommended Outputs

Return the most useful artifact for the request:

- launch plan;
- positioning brief;
- founder-led sales script;
- pricing and onboarding plan;
- launch operating system.
